CDJ Error E-8305: DRM-Protected AAC Files Cannot Play on Pioneer CDJs

You try to play a track you legitimately purchased from the iTunes Store on your CDJ and it immediately shows E-8305 "DATA FORMAT ERROR". The track plays fine in Apple Music on your Mac or iPhone, but no Pioneer CDJ can play it. The reason is FairPlay DRM (Digital Rights Management) — an encryption layer Apple applied to iTunes Store purchases before 2009. DRM-protected files are locked to your Apple ID and cannot be decoded by any third-party hardware.

Technical cause

Apple's iTunes Store sold DRM-protected AAC files in the .m4p format from 2003 until 2009, when Apple switched to DRM-free iTunes Plus files (.m4a). FairPlay DRM encrypts the audio stream inside the MPEG-4 container. Only Apple-authorised software and hardware (iTunes, Apple Music, authorized Macs and iOS devices) can decrypt and play .m4p files. Pioneer CDJs do not implement the FairPlay DRM protocol and cannot decrypt these files — the audio data is unreadable to the CDJ decoder, resulting in E-8305. DRM-protected files use the .m4p extension (note the "p" for protected), while DRM-free AAC files use .m4a. Files purchased after 2009 or upgraded to iTunes Plus are .m4a and DRM-free. Older purchases may still be .m4p unless they were manually upgraded in the iTunes Store. Note: This is distinct from the ALAC issue — both .m4a and .m4p files can trigger E-8305, but for different reasons.

Manual fix (free tools)

There is no direct technical fix for DRM — the encryption cannot be removed without authorisation. The following are the legitimate options available.

1
Upgrade to iTunes Plus (Apple)

If your iTunes Store purchase is still available in the store, Apple may offer a free upgrade to the DRM-free iTunes Plus version. In the Music app on macOS, go to Account → Purchases and check whether an upgrade is available. iTunes Plus files are 256 kbps AAC (.m4a) without DRM and are fully compatible with Pioneer CDJs.

2
Re-purchase as DRM-free

If the upgrade is not available, consider re-purchasing the track from a DRM-free store such as Beatport, Bandcamp, Traxsource, or the iTunes Store (all new purchases since 2009 are DRM-free). Bandcamp and Beatport offer WAV or AIFF downloads for most releases — the best format for CDJ playback.

3
Record to audio (last resort)

On macOS, you can use a virtual audio cable tool (e.g. BlackHole or Soundflower) to route the output of Apple Music to a recording application while the track plays. This produces a real-time audio recording of the DRM-free output. This method produces a complete audio recording at the quality level Apple Music streams (256 kbps AAC or lossless). Whether this is permissible depends on your jurisdiction and the terms of your Apple Music licence.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know if my .m4a or .m4p file is DRM-protected?

Check the file extension: .m4p indicates DRM-protected, .m4a indicates DRM-free (though .m4a may be ALAC — see /cdj-alac-unsupported-fix). In the Music app on macOS, right-click the track → Get Info → File tab. If "Kind" shows "Protected AAC audio file", it is DRM-protected. Alternatively run `ffprobe -v error -i yourfile.m4p` — ffprobe will report a decryption error for protected files.

Can I strip DRM from my iTunes purchases?

Technically it is possible with certain tools, but it is illegal in most jurisdictions under anti-circumvention laws (DMCA in the US, similar laws in the EU). Boothready does not offer or assist with DRM removal. The legitimate path is the iTunes Plus upgrade or re-purchase from a DRM-free store.

I bought the track after 2009 — why is it still DRM-protected?

All iTunes Store purchases after April 2009 should be DRM-free iTunes Plus. If a post-2009 purchase appears as .m4p, it may have been downloaded before the iTunes Plus upgrade was applied to your account. Try deleting the local copy and re-downloading from your Purchased list in the Music app — the re-download should be the DRM-free version.

Does Apple Music lossless streaming work differently from iTunes DRM?

Apple Music lossless downloads (ALAC) are separate from FairPlay DRM-protected purchases. Apple Music subscription downloads are protected by a subscription-based DRM that also cannot be played on CDJs. Only DRM-free purchased files (.m4a iTunes Plus) or files from other sources are CDJ-compatible. See all E-8305 causes at /cdj-error-e-8305.
